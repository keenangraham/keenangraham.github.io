The Epidemiology of Intestinal Protozoa

The Prevalence of Protozoal Infection in 120,000+ Clinical Fecal Samples, Genova Diagnostics, Asheville, N.C., USA, 2014-2015

By Keenan Graham

About

The Parasitology Department at Genova Diagnostics performs ova and parasite microscopy on the fecal samples of doctor-referred patients from all over the world. Between January 2014 and December 2015 Genova resulted over 120,000 of these samples. The large volume of samples, diversity of patient populations, and lack of comprehensive intestinal protozoa prevalence data in the scientific literature, particularly for the United States, make the collection and analysis of these results worthwhile.

Preliminary Results

Here are selected preliminary results, showing the overall shape of the data, and hinting at some of the possibilities of analysis once all of the demographics have been collected. The most interesting results, such as parasite prevalence by location and age/gender variations relative to the infecting organism, are still to come.
