The Epidemiology of Intestinal Parasites

The Prevalence of Protozoal Infection in 100,000+ Clinical Fecal Samples, Genova Diagnostics, Asheville, N.C., USA, 2014-October 2015

By Keenan Graham

About

The Parasitology Department at Genova Diagnostics performs ova and parasite (O&P) microscopy on the fecal samples of doctor-referred patients from all over the world. Since 2014 Genova has resulted over 100,000 of these samples. The large volume of samples, diversity of patient populations, and lack of comprehensive intestinal parasite prevalence data in the scientific literature, particularly for the United States, make the collection and analysis of these results worthwhile.

Since about February of this year I have been thinking a lot about all of the parasitological data that our lab generates. We receive thousands of samples every week from all over the world, although primarily from the United States, Northern Europe, Canada, and Australia. This seems to put us in a privileged and unique position in terms of the volume and geographical distribution of the samples we receive. Most published parasitological surveys have only a tiny fraction of our total numbers and are usually concentrated around a small, manageable area. Therefore, since we are already doing the most labor-intensive parts of a survey, it would be a waste not to investigate and report the resulting data.

Preliminary Results

Here are selected preliminary results, showing the overall shape of the data, and hinting at some of the possibilities of analysis once the initial results have been confirmed for accuracy and all of the demographics have been extracted. So far the results are only about halfway through the systematic review process, and only about a third of the demographics have been collected. The numbers, therefore, will likely change, and the most interesting results, such as prevalence rate by location and age/gender variation relative to the infecting organism(s), are still to come.
